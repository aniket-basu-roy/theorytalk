TheoryTalk

Let's Talk Theory

Download this project as a .zip file Download this project as a tar.gz file

This is a weekly series of informal talks given by people enthusiastic about Theoretical Computer Science. The goal is to have an interactive session discussing papers, book chapters or anything worthwhile that would broaden our common knowledge. The speaker should consider the background of the audience and spend ample time in laying the basics of the material before going on to the nitty-gritties. Also note this is not a platform to prove one's smartness but mutually learn something new.

March 23, 2015

The Lovasz Local Lemma
 Speaker: Ninad Rajgopal

  • The best reference for the probabilistic method - The Probabilistic Method (Alon and Spencer)
    • Chapter 5 talks about Lovasz Local Lemma and it's applications.
  • Another nice reference is this set of lecture notes by Matousek and Vondrak. The Probabilistic Method (Matousek and Vondrak)
  • I could not cover the algorithmic aspects of the LLL. There have been many papers which have looked at the constructive (algorithmic) versions of this technique, with the most famous one being the paper by Moser and Tardos, which gives a constructive proof for the LLL. Hopefully, I might be able to bring it up on a later date.

March 2, 2015

Constructive Discrepancy Minimization by Walking on The Edges.
 Shachar Lovett and Raghu Meka. FOCS 2012
Speaker: Aniket Basu Roy

  • Link to the paper (arXiv)
  • Tutorial video (a rather long one)
  • tcs+ video where the first half discusses the result

The technique of randomly walking in polytopes have been used for developing approximation algorithms as well. The following 2 papers are relevant. We might look at them in the future.

  • Thomas Rothvoss. Approximating Bin Packing within O(log OPT * log log OPT) bins FOCS 2013
  • Nikhil Bansal, Moses Charikar, Ravishankar Krishnaswamy and Shi Li. Better Algorithms and Hardness for Broadcast Scheduling via a Discrepancy Approach SODA 2014

To know more about Discrepancy Theory in general, the following 2 books are available.

  • Bernard Chazelle. The Discrepancy Method
  • Jiri Matousek. Geometric Discrepancy
